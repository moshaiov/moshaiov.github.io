theorem. [Schwarz] let $f:\D\to\D$, $f(0)=0$ be holomorphic. then either $f(z)=e^{i\theta}z$ is a rotation for some angle $\theta\in\R$ or $f$ is a contraction : $|f(z)| < |z|$ for all $z\in\D\-\{0\}$ and $|f'(0)| < 1$.
proof. consider the holomorphic $g:\D\to\C$ given by $g(z)=f(z)/z$ for $z\in\D\-\{0\}$ and $g(0)=f'(0)$. then $g$ is holomorphic. we shall show that $|g|\le 1$. indeed, for $r\in(0,1)$ we have that $\disp\max_{|z|=r}|g(z)|\le 1/r$. by the maximum principle, letting $r\to 1^-$ yields $|g|\le 1$. if at any point $|g(z_0)|=1$ then $g$ is constant and $f$ is a rotation. afa
identity. [Jacobi] $$\sum_{n\ge 1}q^{n^2}a^n = \prod_{k\ge 1} (1-q^{2k}) (1+q^{2k-1}a) (1+q^{2k-1}/a)$$ for all $|q| < 1$, $a\neq 0$.
